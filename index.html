About this Study

What is this study about?

This study aims to explore how social interaction can help reduce sedentary behavior in amongst individuals especially those in research institutions. It aligns with the World Health Organization (WHO) 2020 guidelines, which emphasize the importance of reducing sedentary time to prevent cardiovascular disease, diabetes, cancer, and mental health disorders. Relevant studies (e.g., Ekelund et al., 2019, The Lancet) show that prolonged sitting is a major health risk, and even small increases in physical activity can significantly improve health outcomes.

Why is it relevant?

Sedentary lifestyles have become more common with modern work patterns and remote working. Reducing sitting time—even by a little—can help improve long-term physical and mental health. By participating, you help us better understand how social invitations, and activity tracking can support healthier habits for researchers which is an understudied group.

What is my contribution as a participant?

As a participant, you belong to an elite group of heroes who help in shaping the future of healthier lifestyles. By participating immensely, you'll help us learn how small actions like inviting a friend for coffee or a short walk can disrupt long hours of sitting that often sneak into our daily routines. Your participation means more than just data; it means pioneering a way for people everywhere to live less sedentary, more connected lives.

Thank you for being part of this important journey!

Key Features

Social Activity Invites (Walk/Coffee)

Send spontaneous invites for a quick walk or a coffee break with nearby users to interrupt sitting periods.

Invite and Activity Records

Monitor your overall performance with detailed records: Total Steps, Received, Rejected, Accepted, and Sent Invites.

Pedometer Tracking & Baseline

Track your current steps against your Baseline step count to measure progress in the long-term study.

Privacy Policy

⚠️ **IMPORTANT:** BuddyMove is part of a social behavioural to reduce sedentary behavior, it is not to be a replacement for actual healthy habits. The data collected is handled according to the guidelines of the Daegu Gyeongbuk Institute of Science and Technology Bioethics committee.

1. Information Collection and Use

This policy is used to inform users regarding our policies with the collection, use, and disclosure of information related to our study.

Data We Collect:
  • Personal Information: Name, age, gender, occupation (collected through an evaluation form).
  • Physical Activity Data: Step count data (manually entered during registration and tracked via the phone's built-in pedometer).
  • Location Data (Geolocation): Location is accessed to suggest participants closest to each other for invitation purposes.
  • Usage Data: Information on how the app is accessed and used for analytics for our experimental study.
Purpose of Data Use:

We use your information to evaluate our hypothesis, track and show progress over time, provide tailored recommendations, send and receive social invites, and make inferences and future studies recommendations.

2. Data Storage and Retention

We use secure storage mechanisms (such as encrypted Firebase) to store your data. Your data is not shared with third parties except for essential services (e.g., Firebase for analytics) and only when anonymized.

  • Analysis Data: Anonymized demographic information, physical activity levels, and app usage statistics are retained for the duration of 3 years, as per the recommendation of the University's IRB board, after which they will be discarded responsibly.

3. Your Rights and Data Deletion

You have the right to access, update, or delete your information at any time. You also have the right to **exit this experiment without fault**.

To request the deletion of your information or to withdraw from the study, please contact us via the support email or submit your request through the dedicated form below.
